AED Basics

Background Information

“Women are often less likely to receive bystander CPR because rescuers often fear accusations of inappropriate touching, sexual assault or injuring the victim.” (American Heart Association). Because most CPR and AED demonstration and training are done on male bodies, people are less comfortable touching female breast which can be fetal if a woman undergoes cardiac arrest.

For more information from the American Heart Association

AED and CPR Importance

"Sudden cardiac arrest is among the leading causes of death in the United States. In fact, more than 350,000 people will suffer a cardiac arrest this year. Currently, the only way to restore a regular heart rhythm during cardiac arrest is to use an AED.” (American Heart Association).

AED Step by Step

1) CHECK and CALL
2) Find/turn on AED ASAP
3) Remove clothing and place pads according to AED picture
4) Allow AED to analyze heart rhythm. No one should be touching person at this time
5) Deliver shock if prompted by AED. Do NOT touch person during shock delivery
6) After shock is delivered (or if not shock is advised) begin CPR
